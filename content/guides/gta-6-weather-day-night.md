GTA 6 is bringing the most dynamic weather system Rockstar has ever built. Hurricanes hit Leonida. Heatwaves change how NPCs behave. The time of day affects what is open. Here is everything confirmed.

---

## Day/night cycle confirmed

GTA 6 has a full day/night cycle, shown extensively in both trailers. Vice City at sunset looks dramatically different from Vice City at 2 AM. Specific time-of-day effects confirmed:

- **South Beach clubs open after 10 PM.** The nightlife scene is time-gated.
- **Street races happen at night.** South Beach and Stockyard both mentioned as racing hubs after dark.
- **Neon signage activates at dusk.** Vice City's skyline transforms when the sun sets.
- **NPC routines follow the clock.** Beach crowds during the day, club crowds at night, commuters during rush hours.

---

## Dynamic weather confirmed

Trailer footage and official materials confirm multiple weather states:

### Hurricanes and storms
Trailers show palm trees bending in high winds, sheets of rain, and flooded streets. Rockstar has confirmed weather affects driving, wet roads mean less traction. Strong winds affect aircraft handling.

### Heatwaves
Screenshots show heat shimmer effects over roads and parking lots. NPCs react to extreme heat. This is not cosmetic, it changes NPC density and behavior in outdoor areas during hot periods.

### Fog and humidity
Grassrivers swamps and the Everglades-inspired wetlands show dense morning fog. Visibility changes based on weather. This matters for exploration and possibly for missions.

---

## How weather affects gameplay

This is the part that separates GTA 6 from GTA V. In GTA V, weather was visual. In GTA 6, it changes how the game plays:

| Weather | Gameplay Effect |
|---------|---------------|
| Heavy rain | Reduced driving traction, longer braking distances |
| Hurricane | Possibly closes certain areas, extreme wind effects on vehicles |
| Heatwave | Fewer NPCs outside, more indoor activity, vehicle overheating potential |
| Fog | Reduced visibility, affects air and sea navigation |

---

## What we still do not know

Rockstar has not confirmed:
- Whether hurricanes are scripted story events or random occurrences
- The exact length of the day/night cycle (GTA V was 48 minutes for a full day)
- Whether seasons exist (unlikely, but weather patterns seem more varied than GTA V)
- Whether weather affects mission availability

These details will likely come closer to launch.
