### Gameplay: How They Actually Play

**Meccha Chameleon** is pure mechanical hide-and-seek. You paint yourself to match the environment. The better your painting, the harder you are to find. No tasks to complete. No voting to worry about. hide or hunt. The painting system has surprising depth, roughness, metallic, HSV sliders, pose wheel, clone decoys.

**Among Us** is social deduction disguised as a party game. You run around a spaceship completing simple tasks while one or more impostors secretly kill the crew. The real game happens during emergency meetings, lying, accusing, defending. Victory depends on your ability to manipulate people, not your gaming skills.

**Prop Hunt** (Garry's Mod) is the original. You transform into a random object in the environment and try to blend in. No painting, the game assigns you an object and a position. It's simpler, more random, and more casual than Meccha Chameleon.

### The Bottom Line

**Buy Meccha Chameleon if** you want creative, skill-based hide-and-seek with near-infinite mastery potential. It's the best $6 you'll spend on Steam this year.

**Buy Among Us if** you have a talkative friend group that loves arguing. The game is 20% tasks and 80% social manipulation.

**Play Prop Hunt if** you already own Garry's Mod and want casual, no-pressure fun. It's not worth buying GMod for Prop Hunt.
