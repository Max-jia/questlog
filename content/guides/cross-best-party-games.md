### 1. Meccha Chameleon, $5.99

**The breakout hit of 2026.** Hide-and-seek where you paint yourself to blend into environments. 15 million copies sold, 340K peak players. The painting mechanic has surprising depth (roughness, metallic, silhouette-breaking). Best with 4-12 players on voice chat.

> **Read our full guide:** [How to Play Meccha Chameleon](/games/meccha-chameleon/meccha-chameleon-beginner-guide)

---

### 2. Among Us, $4.99

The game that defined pandemic gaming. Social deduction in space, complete tasks, find the impostor, argue with friends. Cross-platform (PC, mobile, consoles). 15-player lobbies with mod support. Still pulling 25K+ concurrent players years after its peak.

---

### 3. Bongo Cat, Free

A viral rhythm/meme game that exploded to 194K concurrent players. Simple mechanics, absurdly fun with friends. Free-to-play with no pay-to-win. The perfect game for a group that wants to laugh.

---

### 4. Jackbox Party Pack 10, $9.99

Five party games in one pack. Players use their phones as controllers, no extra hardware needed. Trivia, drawing, word games, and bluffing. Works over stream/remote play. The go-to for mixed groups of gamers and non-gamers.

---

### 5. Pico Park 2, $4.99

Cooperative puzzle platformer that tests your communication skills. Each level requires precise teamwork. Expect yelling. 2-8 players. Levels take 5-15 minutes each, perfect for quick sessions.

---

### 6. Golf With Your Friends, $4.99

Mini-golf with physics that range from sensible to chaotic. 12-player lobbies. Workshop support with thousands of custom courses. A relaxed, low-stakes party game that anyone can pick up.

---

### 7. Lethal Company, $9.99

Co-op horror scavenging. Explore abandoned moons, collect scrap, don't die to monsters. Proximity voice chat makes every encounter terrifying and hilarious. 35K+ concurrent players sustained over a year.

---

### 8. PlateUp!, $6.99

Restaurant management roguelike. Build a kitchen, cook food, serve customers, and somehow it's insanely fun with friends. Upgrade your restaurant between runs. 1-4 players. The "one more run" factor is dangerous.

---

### 9. Ultimate Chicken Horse, $7.49

Build a platformer level together, then race to finish it. Place traps and obstacles to sabotage friends while making sure you can still complete it. Pure creative chaos. 4-player local or online.

---

### 10. Move or Die, $4.99

Every 20 seconds, the rules change. Rapid-fire minigames that force constant movement. 4-player madness. Perfect for short, high-energy sessions.

---

### 11. Stick Fight: The Game, $4.99

Physics-based stick figure fighting. 2-8 players. Weapons spawn randomly. Levels collapse. Matches last 30 seconds. The simplicity is the appeal, anyone can play, everyone laughs.

---

### 12. Gang Beasts, $9.99

Wobbly physics brawler. Throw your friends off buildings, into meat grinders, or onto train tracks. Up to 8 players. The jank is the point, it's funnier because it's broken.
