### Glitched Matrix (Easiest)

The only skin that doesn't require any actual gameplay. It's hidden entirely in the UI.

### How to Unlock
1. Go to the **Settings** menu from the main screen.
2. handle to the **Graphics** tab.
3. Set **every single graphics option** to its lowest possible value.
4. Close and reopen the Settings menu **three times**.
5. On the third reopen, a hidden option labeled `[MATRIX]` appears at the bottom.
6. Toggle `[MATRIX]` to ON.
7. Return to the main menu, the Glitched Matrix skin is now available in your character customization.

> **Why this works:** It's an easter egg referencing "glitching the system" by setting everything to minimum. The devs hid it as a reward for players who need to run the game on potato PCs.

### Mecha-Titanium Hazard (Medium)

This one requires collecting hidden data pads across the **Industrial Foundry** map.

### Data Pad Locations

Search for glowing orange tablets in these areas:

| # | Location Detail |
|---|----------------|
| 1 | Beneath the large industrial pipe system on the lower level |
| 2 | Inside a locker in the locker room section |
| 3 | On top of the conveyor belt machinery |
| 4 | Behind hydraulic equipment near the back wall |
| 5 | Elevated structure, requires wall climbing to reach |

Collect all 5 fragments (they don't need to be in a single match). After collecting the last one, you'll receive an encrypted message with the final unlock clue: interact with the main control panel in the center of the Foundry for 10 seconds.

### Neon Specter (Hard)

This skin requires completing a hidden combat challenge in the **Neo-Tokyo Grid** area, without taking any damage.

### How to Unlock
1. Enter any match on a map with the Neo-Tokyo Grid section.
2. Find a specific **balcony near the elevated food district**, it's marked by a flickering neon sign.
3. Interact with the neon signs in this exact color order: **Blue → Pink → Green → Blue → Yellow**. One mistake resets the sequence.
4. A hidden door opens to a zone called **"Endless Alley."**
5. Inside, you'll face a retro-inspired combat encounter. You must **survive without taking a single hit**.
6. Complete the encounter, and Neon Specter unlocks.

### Tips
- Use wall climbing to evade attacks, the ceiling in Endless Alley has safe zones.
- The combat is pattern-based. Learn the attack rhythm before going for the no-hit run.
- Bring a friend to distract enemies (only one player needs to survive hitless).

### Retro Pixel Chameleon (Very Hard)

Arguably the most frustrating skin. You must complete **three challenges in a single match**:

1. **Ghost**, Remain completely undetected for an extended period (approximately 3+ minutes of hunt phase).
2. **Sharpshooter**, Land a long-range elimination as a Seeker (across at least half the map).
3. **Flawless**, Finish the round with full health and no deaths.

### Strategy
- Play on **Penguin Hotel**, it's the best map for staying hidden.
- For the Sharpshooter requirement, wait until you're a Seeker in a round where a Hider is in an obvious elevated position. Don't rush the shot.
- This may take many attempts. Don't get discouraged.

### Prismatic Overlord (Legendary, The Rarest Skin)

The ultimate status symbol in Meccha Chameleon. Requires a coordinated team.

### How to Unlock
1. Assemble a team of **at least 4 players** with high combined levels.
2. Go to the **community hub** area.
3. Solve an activation puzzle, each player must stand on a specific pressure plate in the correct order. The plates glow faintly when stepped on.
4. This summons a **hidden boss encounter** featuring:
 - Large-scale dodge mechanics
 - Puzzle-solving sections
 - Team coordination phases
 - Shield-breaking objectives
 - Multi-stage combat
5. Defeat the boss to unlock the skin.

### Finding a Team
- Use the Meccha Chameleon Discord server to find groups attempting the Prismatic Overlord unlock.
- The boss fight is designed for 4+ players, solo is impossible.
