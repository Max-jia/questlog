## How You'll Make Money in GTA 6

GTA V's economy was simple: do heists, get paid. GTA 6 is building a deeper crime economy with multiple income streams, fencing, raiding, restoring, and (of course) heisting. Here's everything confirmed.

---

### Heists, The Big Scores

Raul Bautista, a veteran bank robber, is your heist connection. He recruits Jason and Lucia for multi-stage jobs involving:
- Planning and casing the target
- Recruiting a crew with specific skills
- Executing the heist (approach likely matters, stealth vs loud)
- Splitting the payout

If this follows GTA V's heist model (which was the best-received feature), expect 5-6 major heists across the story, each unlocking progressively. The Ultimate Edition's "gang-compound raid" is likely a bonus heist.

---

### Fencing Stolen Goods, The Passive Income

This is new. Multiple sources confirm:
- Garages and safehouses have **stolen goods storage**
- You deposit items from thefts and robberies at these locations
- A **fence** buys them from you

This implies the return of **burglary missions** (last seen in GTA: San Andreas). Breaking into houses, stealing valuables, and selling them through a network of fences creates a passive crime income loop between major heists.

---

### Gang Compound Raids, Combat-Focused Income

A confirmed activity type. You assault gang hideouts (PTT Youngin$ compound is named), eliminate hostiles, and extract with contraband. Think of these as smaller, more frequent, combat-focused versions of heists, perfect for making money between story missions.

---

### Classic Car Restoration, The Legit Money

Collector Wyman's "Special Commission": find abandoned classic cars hidden across Leonida, restore them, and sell for profit. This is GTA's take on Forza Horizon's barn find system. A rare legitimate income source in a game about crime.

---
