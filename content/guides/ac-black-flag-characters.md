Black Flag Resynced has one of the best-written casts in the Assassin's Creed series. Here is every major character — who they are, what they want, and why they matter to the story.

---

## The Pirates (Your Allies)

### Edward Kenway

**Role**: Protagonist. Welsh privateer turned pirate turned Assassin.

**Backstory**: Edward was a poor sailor who married wealthy Caroline Scott against her parents' wishes. He left for the Caribbean to make his fortune as a privateer, got shipwrecked, and stole the robes of a dead Assassin to claim a reward. This single impulsive act — impersonating an Assassin — drives the entire plot.

**Character arc**: Edward starts as a selfish treasure hunter. He watches his friends die (Blackbeard, Mary Read, several crew members) and slowly realizes gold does not fill the void. By the end, he joins the Assassins voluntarily — not for money, but because he finally believes in their cause. The post-credits scene shows him as a father, redeemed.

**Gameplay relevance**: All upgrades and ship progression go through Edward. His four pistol holsters are the most important combat upgrade.

---

### Edward "Blackbeard" Thatch

**Role**: Legendary pirate captain, Edward's mentor figure.

**Backstory**: The real Blackbeard (Edward Teach) was the most feared pirate in the Caribbean. In the game, he is older, wiser, and tired of the pirate life. He wants to retire in peace but keeps getting pulled back in.

**Why he matters**: Blackbeard's death in Sequence 9 is the game's emotional low point. He goes down fighting an entire British naval squadron alone on the Queen Anne's Revenge. His final words — "In a world without gold, we might have been heroes" — define the game's central theme.

**Historical accuracy note**: The real Blackbeard died in 1718 in a battle with Lieutenant Robert Maynard, which the game recreates faithfully. The Resynced version adds an extended boarding sequence not present in the original 2013 game.

---

### Mary Read / James Kidd

**Role**: Pirate, Assassin, Edward's moral compass.

**Backstory**: Born female, Mary lived as a man ("James Kidd") to serve in the British military and later as a pirate. She is a trained Assassin who tries to recruit Edward.

**Why she matters**: Mary's death in prison (Sequence 9) is the turning point of Edward's character arc. She dies of fever while Edward escapes, and her last speech — begging Edward to be a better man — is the moment he stops being a selfish pirate and starts becoming an Assassin.

**Historical accuracy note**: The real Mary Read was also a female pirate who disguised herself as male and died in prison of fever in 1721. The game's timeline compresses this slightly.

---

### Adewale

**Role**: Edward's quartermaster, later a major character in AC Freedom Cry (the standalone DLC included in Resynced).

**Backstory**: Adewale was freed from a slave ship by Edward and became his most trusted friend. He is the moral voice of the Jackdaw, constantly pushing Edward to help the oppressed rather than just chase gold.

**Why he matters**: Adewale represents the Assassin ideals that Edward eventually embraces. He is also the protagonist of the Freedom Cry DLC, which takes place 15 years after Black Flag's main story and has its own powerful narrative about slave liberation in Haiti.

---

### Charles Vane

**Role**: Ruthless pirate captain, eventual antagonist.

**Backstory**: The real Charles Vane was known for cruelty even by pirate standards. In the game, he starts as a reluctant ally but betrays Edward multiple times. He represents what Edward could become if he never finds a moral compass.

**Narrative function**: Vane is the mirror — a vision of the selfish pirate Edward almost becomes. Their final confrontation is not just physical but philosophical.

---

## The Templars (Your Enemies)

### Laureano de Torres y Ayala

**Role**: Grand Master of the Caribbean Templars, governor of Florida.

**What he wants**: Torres is the primary antagonist. He seeks the Observatory — a First Civilization device that can spy on anyone through a drop of their blood. Unlike most Templar villains, Torres is not mustache-twirling evil. He genuinely believes the Observatory will bring order to a chaotic world.

**Final boss note**: Torres is the final boss of the main story (Sequence 12). The fight takes place inside the Observatory with unique environmental hazards not seen anywhere else in the game.

---

### Bartholomew Roberts / The Sage

**Role**: The Sage — a reincarnated First Civilization being. Pirate captain, Templar ally, then betrayer of both sides.

**What he wants**: Roberts (as Sage) holds the genetic memories of Aita, a First Civilization scientist. He knows the Observatory's location and how to operate it. He manipulates Edward, the Templars, and everyone else to serve his own ends.

**Why he matters to the AC meta-story**: The Sages appear across multiple AC games. Roberts is the second Sage encountered in the series (the first was in AC3). His existence confirms that the First Civilization's influence extends into the 18th century and beyond. If you care about AC lore, pay close attention to every scene Roberts is in.

---

### Woodes Rogers

**Role**: Templar, governor of the Bahamas, Edward's personal antagonist.

**Backstory**: A real historical figure — Rogers was sent by the British crown to clean up the pirate republic of Nassau. In the game, he is a Templar who uses his political power to destroy the pirates' way of life.

**Why he matters**: Rogers is the face of Templar order versus pirate freedom. Unlike Torres (who is distant and philosophical), Rogers is hands-on and personal. He hunts Edward directly.
