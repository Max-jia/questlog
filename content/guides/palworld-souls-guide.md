Souls are permanent stat boosts for your Pals. Once applied, you cannot undo them. This means every soul you spend on a Pal you will replace later is a soul wasted. Here is how to farm them and where to spend them.

---

### The five soul types

| Soul | Stat Boost | Priority |
|------|-----------|:---:|
| Attack Soul | Damage output | 1 |
| Defense Soul | Damage reduction | 2 |
| HP Soul | Health pool | 3 |
| Stamina Soul | Stamina pool | 4 |
| Work Speed Soul | Base efficiency | 5 (skip) |

Attack first. Defense second. HP third. Work Speed is almost never worth it — your base Pals do not need souls to do their jobs.

---

### Where to farm souls

### Dungeon chests
The most reliable source. Every dungeon has at least one chest with souls. Higher level dungeons drop larger souls. Run dungeons whenever you see them.

### High-level Pals
Pals above level 30 have a chance to drop souls when defeated or captured. Not every Pal. Not every time. But enough to matter.

### Wildlife Sanctuary
The floating islands have chests with better drop rates. More dangerous, better rewards.

### Do not buy souls
The wandering merchant sells souls. They are absurdly expensive. The gold cost is not worth it. Farm dungeons instead.

---

### Which Pals to soul first

Only soul Pals you will keep forever. Your main combat team. Your raid team. Legendaries you spent hours catching.

Do not soul:
- Early-game Pals you will replace
- Base workers (Work Speed souls are weak anyway)
- Pals you have not bred for perfect passives yet

The full progression order: breed for passives → apply mutations → condense → soul → awaken. Souls come after you are sure this Pal is a keeper.
