Money in Moonlight Peaks comes from four sources: farming, livestock, the Nokturna card game, and mining for gems. Here is how much each earns, the optimal strategy for each, and how to stack them for maximum gold.

---

## Income Source Comparison

| Source | Work Type | Est. Weekly Income | Startup Cost |
|--------|----------|:---:|:---:|
| Frostberry Greenhouse (winter) | Daily harvest | 10,700G | 5,000G + materials |
| Vampire Vine farming (fall) | Daily harvest | 4,000G | 500G for seeds |
| Draculambs + Hellkittens | Passive (daily collect) | 2,900G | 7,000G for 4 animals |
| Nokturna Card Game | Active (weekly) | 3,000-7,500G | 0G (starter deck) |
| Gem Mining | Active (mining runs) | 2,000-5,000G per run | 300G for pickaxe upgrade |
| Fishing | Active | 1,000-2,000G | 0G |
| Quest Rewards | Active (one-time) | 500-3,000G each | Varies |

**The optimal income strategy**: Farm + Livestock (passive daily income) + Nokturna (weekly income) + Seasonal mining runs (when you need specific gems). This earns 15,000-20,000G per in-game week by mid-game.

---

## Phase 1: Early Game Money (First Season, Spring)

Starting gold: 500G.

**Nights 1-7**: Plant 20 Nightshade. Harvest every 2 nights. Sell all of it (900G per harvest, 5,400G total by night 7). Do not process into potions yet — raw Nightshade is worth more early game when you need cash for seeds and tools.

**Nights 8-14**: Buy 2 Draculambs (1,500G × 2 = 3,000G). Switch crops to Ghost Peppers (highest spring profit). Keep 10 Nightshade for animal feed. By night 14, your daily income is: 280G from Ghost Peppers + 240G from Blood Wool (2 Draculambs, every 3 nights averages to ~80G/day each) = ~520G per night.

**Nights 15-28**: Buy 2 Hellkittens (2,000G × 2 = 4,000G). Upgrade the watering can to copper. By end of spring, you should have 4 animals, copper tools, and ~8,000G saved for summer.

---

## Phase 2: Mid-Game Scaling (Summer-Fall)

**Goal**: Save 15,000G for the greenhouse by end of fall.

**Summer**: Plant Moonlit Melons (highest summer profit). Befriend Luna to 5 hearts for the Growth Surge spell (force-harvest melons). With Growth Surge, your effective harvest rate per melon doubles — one melon every 3.5 nights instead of 7.

**Fall**: Plant Vampire Vines. They require trellises (wood + iron, cheap). Vampire Vines also drop Void Essence as a rare bonus (10% chance per harvest), which sells for 800G.

**Greenhouse timing**: If you follow this pace, you will have the greenhouse built by mid-fall. Immediately plant Frostberries. The greenhouse pays for itself in 12 nights.

**Income breakdown with full setup (Winter)**:

| Source | Per Night | Per Week |
|--------|:---:|:---:|
| Frostberry Greenhouse (24 plots) | 1,536G | 10,752G |
| Draculambs (2) + Hellkittens (2) + Wraith Cow (1) | ~500G | 3,500G |

At this income level, you can buy every upgrade, every recipe, and every decoration in the game within 2 seasons. Money stops mattering. At that point, focus on completing the Seven Families storyline and maxing out relationships.

## Nokturna Money: The Forgotten Gold Mine

As covered in the Beginner Guide, the Nokturna card game earns 200-500G per win per NPC per week. Here is the town-wide route optimized for time:

**Nokturna Night (Monday)**: Every NPC resets their challenge on Monday (in-game). Dedicate Monday night to card games:
1. Start at the Howling Wolf Tavern (4 werewolves) → 800-2,000G
2. Walk to Luna's Potion Shop (2 witches) → 400-1,000G
3. Fly to Town Square (5 humans) → 1,000-2,500G
4. Finish at Mermaid Cove (2 mermaids, requires diving) → 400-1,000G

Total Monday haul: 2,600-6,500G in about 2 in-game hours. Spend the rest of the week farming and doing whatever you want — Nokturna is a single-night weekly commitment.

**The deck to use**: Once you have access to rare cards (buy from the traveling merchant on Fridays), build this deck:
- 5x Bat Shield (defense, 2 cost)
- 5x Crimson Heal (healing, 3 cost)
- 5x Shadow Strike (attack, 3 cost)
- 3x Vampiric Drain (attack + heal, 5 cost)
- 2x Full Moon (doubles all damage for 3 turns, 7 cost)

Strategy: defend + heal until you reach 10 mana → Full Moon → Shadow Strike spam for 3 turns. Wins against every AI except the tournament champion.

## What NOT to Spend Money On

1. **Sword upgrades**. You can clear all combat content with Shadow Bolt + Mist Form. The sword is a cosmetic weapon.
2. **House decorations early game**. Your farmhouse does not generate income. Upgrade it last.
3. **The traveling merchant's random items**. 90% are overpriced. Only buy Crystal Balls (500G, needed for Witch Coven quest) and rare Nokturna cards.
4. **Prepared meals from the bakery**. Olivia charges 200G for a dish you can cook yourself for 50G of ingredients.
5. **The "Expand Coffin" upgrade**. You do not need a king-size coffin. The Travel Coffin is the only coffin upgrade that matters (place it anywhere outside).
