Most players build the Scanner Station, use it once, and forget about it. That is a mistake. It is the single best tool for cutting down tedious searching. Here is how to use it properly.

---

## How to unlock the Scanner Station

Scan 3 fragments in wrecks around Coral Gardens. Once unlocked, build it in your base:

- 1 System Chip
- 3 Titanium
- 1 Wiring Kit

Place it in a room with power. It does not need its own room. It works from anywhere in range.

---

## What the Scanner Station can track

The station has multiple scanning modes. Each takes a few seconds to complete and highlights all matching targets within range:

- **Resources:** Titanium, Copper, Silver, Gold, Lithium, every ore and material node lights up on your HUD
- **Fragments:** Blueprint fragments, wreckage, data boxes
- **Creatures:** Hostile and passive fauna. Useful for avoiding Leviathans or finding specific fish
- **Flora:** Plants for farming and crafting

Pick one mode. Scan. The results stay on your HUD for about 10 seconds. Scan again when they fade.

---

## Range upgrades

The Scanner Station accepts range upgrade modules. Each module increases the scan radius. With 4 modules, one station covers most of a biome.

This is especially useful in Karakorum where Celestine and Conduit Crystal are spread across a large area and hard to spot against the dark terrain.

---

## One scan per lobby

If you are in multiplayer, the Scanner Station tracks one resource node at a time for the entire lobby. Coordinate with your team. Do not have two players scanning for different things simultaneously. It overwrites.

---

## Best places to build dedicated scanner outposts

Build small scanner outposts (1 room, Scanner Station, power source) at:
- Edge of the Shallows (covers Shallows + Jelly Barrens)
- Near the Old Habitat (covers Silver and lead deposits)
- At the trench entrance to Karakorum (covers Celestine and Conduit Crystal)

Each outpost costs a few Titanium and a System Chip. The time they save pays for itself within one expedition.
