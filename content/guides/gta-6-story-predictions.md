### What We Know for Sure (From Rockstar)

Rockstar's official description: *"A simple job goes wrong, pulling the duo into a state-wide criminal conspiracy. They must rely on each other more than ever."*

**The confirmed setup:**
- Lucia is released from Leonida Penitentiary at the start (shown in Trailer 1 with ankle monitor)
- Jason has a military background and connections to drug runners in the Florida Keys
- Raul Bautista is recruiting for "big scores", your heist connection
- The story uses RDR2-style chapter progression
- It's a love story, more intimate than GTA V's three-protagonist sprawl

---

### Major Plot Theories

### Theory 1: The "One Last Score" Arc
Jason and Lucia start small, robberies, petty crime. Raul Bautista recruits them for increasingly larger heists. The final job goes wrong. One of them is forced to betray the other (Rockstar loves this, GTA V, RDR2 both have betrayal arcs).

**Evidence:** Raul is described as a veteran bank robber. The "simple job gone wrong" setup is classic GTA. The Bonnie & Clyde comparison suggests things end badly.

---

### Theory 2: Lucia's Parole Is the Ticking Clock
Lucia starts the game on parole (ankle monitor). Law enforcement is watching her. Every crime she commits, with Jason, risks sending her back to prison. This creates natural tension: do they go straight, or go bigger and risk everything?

**Evidence:** Trailer 1 opens with Lucia in prison. The ankle monitor is visible in multiple scenes.

---

### Theory 3: The Vice City Nostalgia Connection
The Vercetti Estate exists. Phil Cassidy appears on TV. Tommy Vercetti's Hawaiian shirt is a weapon skin. Something happened to Tommy's empire between 1986 and 2026, and uncovering that history may be part of the story.

**Evidence:** The Hawk & Little Morgan revolvers are sourced from Vercetti Estate. Rockstar is deliberately connecting the universes.

### How GTA Stories Usually End

Rockstar doesn't do purely happy endings:

| Game | Ending |
|------|--------|
| GTA: Vice City | Tommy succeeds, kills Lance Vance, becomes kingpin. Bittersweet win. |
| GTA: San Andreas | CJ kills Big Smoke and Tenpenny. Family reunited. Relatively happy. |
| GTA IV | Player chooses: Roman dies or Kate dies. Niko loses someone. |
| GTA V | Option C (Deathwish) is canon, all three survive. |
| **RDR2** | Arthur dies. John gets his redemption, temporarily. |

**Pattern:** GTA games let you win, but at a cost. RDR2 showed Rockstar is willing to kill protagonists. Jason and Lucia's Bonnie & Clyde setup strongly suggests tragedy.
